Win Number Calculator

What is the Win Number Calculator?

The Win Number Calculator is a tool that takes a few statistics about voting in your district and calculates your Win Number - the estimated number of votes you need to win. In a numbers-driven campaign, it's important to have a win number and to work towards it. A lot of field organizers spend a ton of time working on numbers before they've recruited enough volunteers to hit one tenth of their goals. So this win number calculator is a way to save you time and get you a ballpark estimate.

What is Turnout History?

The Turnout History is the number of residents who voted in your district in past general elections.

What is a Turnout Rate?

The Turnout Rate is the percentage of voters who are registered to vote at the time of the election that turned out and voted in a past election.

How do I determine my Turnout History?

The Turnout History is publicly available data that is tracked by your state Board of Elections or your Secretary of State. It is usually available online.

What if my district has not had 3 elections yet due to redistricting?

You have a couple of options.

  1. You could include a single year’s data multiple times.
  2. If your district is similar enough to a previously existing district, then you can use the data from the old district.
  3. For a drastically changed district, you can break down the district by precinct, and add up the turn out for each, divided by the number of residents who voted. This is a few hours work, even if you're good with a spreadsheet. If you are the candidate, get someone to do this for you.

How do I determine the number of registered voters in your district?

The number of registered voters in your district is publicly available data that is tracked by your state Board of Elections or your Secretary of State. It is usually available online at your Secretary of State, State Board of Elections, or Government Accountability Board website.

What do I use my Win Number for?

Your Win Number is what campaigners also refer to as 50% + 1, the number of votes you need for victory. It is the basis for a numbers-based campaign and allows your to calculate all your field goals such as IDs, door knocks, contact made, and GOTV targeting.

The Win Number is also great for motivating you and your team If you have 15,000 votes to win you can't run a campaign like a 3,000-vote candidates. If it's your plan to knock on every door in the district, having a win number helps you remember just how many doors you have to go.
