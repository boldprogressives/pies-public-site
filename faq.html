Frequently Asked Questions

PIES

What does it do?

It does many things, all to make life easier to setup and run a data driven given campaign using best practices.

Is it free?

YES! PIES is free to progressive candidates. Please check out our User Agreement. If you are using our Website Builder tool, you will be responsible for domain registration and hosting fees - but don't worry we've picked out providers that give the best rates around.

Website Builder

Visual Editor

What is the Visual Editor?

The Visual Editor is where you make your website yours. It contains all the tools to create content, upload images, configure, and preview your new website.

How do I preview my site?

The preview is built into the Visual Editor. When you make a change, click the Preview button under the option you changed. If you want to keep the change click Save button. To fully see the site, you can hide the toolbar by clicking the purple Hide tab on the site. You can also move the toolbar by changing the Editor View in the foot of the toolbar.

What about mobile visitors?

Some numbers show that over 50% of visitors to campaign websites come from mobile devices. The good news is that your website with PIES is mobile ready. PIES website are responsive meaning that they are responsive, it is designed to be the easy to use, no mater what device a visitor uses. To check out the responsiveness of your site, check out the Site View options in the toolbar footer. It will show you how your site will look on any device.

Visual Editor - Theme

What does the Color Scheme option do?

Every well designed website has a color palette which defines the dominant colors of the site. You can choose from a set of predefined palettes or even better, you can create your own! If you have an image file of your campaign logo you can use a palette generator.

These are some popular ones:

  • Pictaculous
  • DeGraeve Color Palette
  • Five Amazing Color Palette Generators

Or you can build your own palette:

  • Color Scheme Designer

What is the Background Image?

The Background Image creates the backdrop for your front page. Select from one of the many options to find a backdrop that fits your district, or you can upload an image to use. Crowd shots or a photograph taken during a site visit are great choices.

Visual Editor - Images

What should I use for the Campaign Logo?

The campaign logo is the graphic your campaign uses to brand the candidate and therefor is an important part of branding your website. Most campaigns will contract a graphic artist to design the logo. They will often use a dark background color, with white (or light) text for the logo. We also recommend you use a .gif or .png with a transparent background. For best results, use an image that's 480px wide, and not too tall. The logo image will be placed in the upper right hand corner of the homepage. Try out different sizes for your logo to find the one that fits best. If you do not specify a logo, that space will be filled with the Campaign Name specified in the Content section.

What is the Splash Image?

The Splash Image is the image that will dominate the center of your homepage. Make sure that the image is not too tall so that it fits well, and does not make the page too long. For best results, use a .jpg that's 800px wide. Landscape-style shots only, no tall/thin images / portraits. Ideal for the space is actually a high-quality family photo or exciting campaign-trail shot like the candidate speaking at a rally.

What is the Optional Caption for?

The Optional Caption is text that will overlay the Splash Image. The caption is a great place for a tagline for the candidate. Doesn’t use the Optional Caption if your Splash Image already has text in it. The caption will obscure the text, making both difficult to read.

Visual Editor - Content

What is the Campaign Name?

The Campaign name has 2 pieces - the candidate’s name and the office she is running for. It is used as a placeholder for the logo until your campaign has a logo image, and is also used at the footer of the page.

What is the Why I’m Running text?

This is your candidate’s elevator pitch. It should follow the 27-9-3 rule. 27 words or less that can be repeated in about 9 seconds which conveys 3 main points about why the candidate is running. It is used as the appeal above the signup form.

How is the Candidate Biography used?

If a Candidate Biography is specified, then a new link will be added to the navigation to a new page of the site, the About Page.

What is the Legal Disclaimer?

The Legal Disclaimer is the legal text required to be on most campaign materials. If the office is federal, it should have the format “Paid for by” then the official name of the campaign that was used when filing with the FEC. If is a state or lower office, the Secretary of State or Board of Elections may have their own disclosure rules requiring a similar text.

Visual Editor - News

How do I create a News page for the website?

All you need to do to create a New page is to add New Article under the News section of the toolbar.

How do I create a News Article?

First click on the News bar to open the News section of the toolbar, then click the Add New Article link. A form to specify the details of your article will appear.

Do I have to have an Author or a Publication Date for my News Article?

None of the fields are required, but we recommend at least specifying a Title and the link to the article or add multimedia. Try to be consistent with which fields you do use.

How do I add multimedia to a News Article?

You can add video, audio or an image to a News Article.The Visual Editor supports video files uploaded to YouTube, audio files uploaded to SoundCloud, or you can upload an image. Select the type of of media you want to link to in the dropdown and the options necessary to specify the media file. For audio and video, you’ll need to pull the id off of the respective URL.

How do I delete an article?

In the News section, under each article is a trash can icon. Click to delete the news article to be removed.

How do you remove the News link from the navigation bar?

The News link gets removed once all the news articles have been deleted.

Visual Editor - Issues

How to add a new issue?

Adding an issue is easy, first click on the Issues bar to open the Issues section of the toolbar, then click the Add New Issue link. An Issue Title field and Issue Content textarea will get displayed for you to add information about a new issue the candidate has a position on. Once you preview and save an Issue, the Issues link we be added to the navigation bar and a new Issues page will be created.

How do I delete an issue?

In the Issues section, under each Issue is a trash can icon. Click to delete the issue to be removed.

How do you remove the Issues link from the navigation bar?

The Issues link gets removed once all the issues have been deleted.

How can I change the order of the Issues on my Issues page?

Unfortunately you can't right now. We're working on it! Feel free to use the "help" button and let us know if it's a priority for you.

How do I collect online donations?

A strong base of small donors is a critical piece of of a good finance plan. There are lots of options for payment processing. ActBlue is the recommended provider of donation pages. You can create pages for free, and ActBlue is constantly analyzing their page performance for the best possible conversion rates, aka the rate in which a user converts from a visitor to a contact by submitting a form. The Website Builder can integrate with any donation page you choose. Other popular tools to collect online donations include Salsa, NGP, Blue State Digital, ActionKit and Nation Builder, however these are commercial products that cost money.

How do I put a donation button on my website?

To add the donate button, click the Links bar in the toolbar to open the Links section. Under Donate Page copy and paste the URL of the contribution page you have created.

How do I change the text of my donate button?

If you’d like to change the text of the button to something other than “Donate”, specify it in the field after the Donate Link.

How do I track my site’s statistics?

Knowing when you are getting traffic spikes to your website is an important part of running a data driven campaign. You can easily (and for free!) sign up for a Google Analytics account at http://www.google.com/analytics/

Once you do, Google will generate a Google Analytics Key. Copy and paste that key into the Google Analytics field in the Links section. This will automatically add the code necessary to start collecting all your web stats.

How do I build followers on Twitter?

Specifying a Twitter Name will cause the Twitter follow button to be displayed over the sign up form, and adds the Twitter link to the footer.

What does the Facebook Page field do?

Specifying a Facebook Page will cause the Facebook Like button to be displayed over the sign up form, and adds the Facebook link to the footer.

Win Number Calculator

What is the Win Number Calculator?

The Win Number Calculator is a tool that takes a few statistics about voting in your district and calculates your Win Number - the estimated number of votes you need to win. In a numbers-driven campaign, it's important to have a win number and to work towards it. A lot of field organizers spend a ton of time working on numbers before they've recruited enough volunteers to hit one tenth of their goals. So this win number calculator is a way to save you time and get you a ballpark estimate.

What is Turnout History?

The Turnout History is the number of residents who voted in your district in past general elections.

What is a Turnout Rate?

The Turnout Rate is the percentage of voters who are registered to vote at the time of the election that turned out and voted in a past election.

How do I determine my Turnout History?

The Turnout History is publicly available data that is tracked by your state Board of Elections or your Secretary of State. It is usually available online.

What if my district has not had 3 elections yet due to redistricting?

You have a couple of options.

  1. You could include a single year’s data multiple times.
  2. If your district is similar enough to a previously existing district, then you can use the data from the old district.
  3. For a drastically changed district, you can break down the district by precinct, and add up the turn out for each, divided by the number of residents who voted. This is a few hours work, even if you're good with a spreadsheet. If you are the candidate, get someone to do this for you.

How do I determine the number of registered voters in your district?

The number of registered voters in your district is publicly available data that is tracked by your state Board of Elections or your Secretary of State. It is usually available online at your Secretary of State, State Board of Elections, or Government Accountability Board website.

What do I use my Win Number for?

Your Win Number is what campaigners also refer to as 50% + 1, the number of votes you need for victory. It is the basis for a numbers-based campaign and allows your to calculate all your field goals such as IDs, door knocks, contact made, and GOTV targeting.

The Win Number is also great for motivating you and your team If you have 15,000 votes to win you can't run a campaign like a 3,000-vote candidates. If it's your plan to knock on every door in the district, having a win number helps you remember just how many doors you have to go.

Call Time Tracker

Log Activity

What does the Log Activity tab do?

The Log Activity tab is where you record each day’s call time results.

How do I add a new day’s call time result?

You can add a day’s result in the Add New Data section at the top of the Log Activity tab. To Save the data, click the Add button to the right. This is the data you need to specify:

  • Date - the date that the calls were made
  • Calls - the number of phone numbers dialed
  • Connects - the number of calls that resulted in the desired person answering the phone, whether this was a full conversation with an ask included, or something less successful. Connect rates help you track whether you're calling at the right times of day and using good lists.
  • Collected via Phone - the amount of money generated by your calls that was submitted via an online fundraising page that day
  • Hard Pledged - the amount of money that has been firmly committed to during the call time. Only use this field when the ask was for a specific amount and the donor replied with a 'yes'. Soft pledges are not currently tracked in the call time tracker.
  • Hours Completed - the total number of hours spent calling for the day
  • Hours Scheduled - the total number of hours the candidate was initially scheduled to call that day

To edit the data for a day that has already been entered, go to the Past Activity table and click the Edit button on the end of the row with that day’s date. The data for that day will fill the fields of the Add New Data section, where you can change the values you wish to edit, and then click the Add button to save. Also, in the "Add New Data" section, if you select the day you want to edit, it will load up that day's data in the form and you can save it back with changes.

How do I delete a day’s data record?

To delete the data for a day that has already been entered, got to the Past Activity table and click the delete (garbage can) icon, then confirm you would like it removed.

When I click edit, nothing seems to happen. What’s going on?

You likely clicked edit on an empty row. When you click on edit, it fills the fields in the Add New Data section with that row. If the row is empty, only the Date will get filled.

Help! How can I export my data?

Don’t worry -- exporting your data is super simple. Just highlight the rows you want in the Past Activity table, then copy and paste into a spreadsheet program of your choice.

Dashboard

What are goals?

Goals are the amount of pledges you aspire to collect during call time for this week, this quarter, or over the life of your campaign. Setting goals allows you to track your progress and determine what kind of changes you need to fine tune your fundraising efforts.

What happened to my goal from this past week or this past quarter?

Weekly and quarterly goals automatically get cleared when a new week or quarter starts.

What is the “pledged this week” goal?

This is the total of Hard Pledges and money Collected Online you expect to collect during call time this week. A week runs from Monday through Sunday.

Why are my metrics 0?

If you have no data logged for the time period specified, This Week of This Quarter, then your metrics will be zero.

How do I get my call time totals for this week, this quarter, or for the life of the campaign?

Under the Detailed Progress section of the Dashboard tab, you can specify totals in the Progress For dropdown. The table below will break out your call time metrics by the grouping you specify in the dropdown on the left. The totals will appear at the bottom of the table in bold.

How do I get my call time totals for a specific week, quarter, or year?

Under the Detailed Progress section of the Dashboard tab, in the dropdown to the left of the Progress For dropdown you can select a particular time period you would like totaled at the bottom of the table in bold. The Send Reports tab is also great for getting totals.

Can I sort my results by Calls, Connects, etc.?

Yes! Just click on the column heading to sort.

Send Reports

How do I generate a report of my call time progress?

On the Send Report tab, click the green button called Generate Report. The report will appear in the box below, where you can edit and copy into an email.

What are the different Report Types?

On the Send Reports tab, the Call Time Tracker offers Daily, Weekly, and Quarterly reports that can be selected by the Report Type dropdown. By default, the report will default to the current date. To generate a report other than the current day, week, or quarter, specify the start date of the period in the Select Date field.

How do I send my report?

After clicking the Generate Report button, your report will be displayed in the section below. You can use the Copy To Clipboard button to select all and copy the report, then paste into a document or email of your choice.
